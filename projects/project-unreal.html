Overview

These Unreal Engine projects were made as school projects, and each one focused on a different objective. The goal was not to create fully polished games, but to experiment with new systems and get more comfortable with Unreal Engine.

Arena Shooter Prototype was my first real experience with Unreal and Blueprint. It was a group project where we had to build a simple arena shooter with multiple weapons and enemy types.

I worked in a team of four and took a technical lead role. I helped organize the project and made sure no one was stuck for too long on technical problems. At the same time, I worked mainly on the enemy system: AI behaviour, different enemy types, and spawning logic using Behavior Trees and Blackboards.

N.A.J.I.M. was a second project focused on multiplayer. This time I worked solo and decided to recreate a small co-op game inspired by R.E.P.O., where players collect objects while avoiding an enemy.

This project was my introduction to networking in Unreal. I implemented basic multiplayer systems like grabbing objects, shared progression, and server/client synchronization.

These projects are still prototypes, but they helped me understand Unreal much better, especially for AI and multiplayer systems.

Project Goals

  • Learn how to use Unreal Engine and Blueprint.
  • Create simple AI using Behavior Trees and Blackboards.
  • Work in a team and take technical responsibilities.
  • Understand the basics of multiplayer in Unreal.
  • Build small but playable prototypes.
